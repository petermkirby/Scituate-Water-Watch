What public records show about Scituate's water

Sourced from EPA SDWIS, Massachusetts DEP, and the Scituate Water Division's own reporting — including where those sources disagree.

The system

The Scituate Water Division, run by the Town's Department of Public Works, serves an estimated 19,760 residents. Unlike most South Shore systems drawing from a single watershed, Scituate blends five distinct sources: six active groundwater wells, Old Oaken Bucket Pond, the Tack Factory Pond/Reservoir system, and — for the Humarock section of town specifically — water purchased under an agreement with the neighboring Town of Marshfield.

Public utility records list the primary supply as surface water, meaning the ponds and reservoir carry significant weight in the overall mix alongside the wells.

Violation history: where the public trackers disagree

This is the most important thing we can tell you about Scituate's data: independent public trackers do not agree on the system's violation history, and we think that disagreement is worth being upfront about rather than papering over.

These are third-party aggregations of EPA SDWIS data, not EPA's database itself, and they clearly don't match. Rather than presenting one as fact, we recommend checking EPA's ECHO database directly, or requesting Scituate's official Consumer Confidence Report from the Water Division at (781) 545-8735.

PFAS testing

Unlike some neighboring systems, public tracking data indicates Scituate's PFAS monitoring has not shown compounds above EPA's health-based guidelines as of the latest reporting available to us. That's a genuinely different picture than towns where PFAS has turned up above limits, and we think it's worth saying plainly rather than manufacturing concern where the data doesn't support it.

Massachusetts requires ongoing PFAS monitoring through 2029 under EPA's finalized PFAS drinking water rules, so this is a status check, not a permanent conclusion — we'll update this page as new results are published.
